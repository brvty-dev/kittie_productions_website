Legal Obligations

Those working with Kittie Productions in any capacity are required to familiarise themselves with the following obligations.

Modern Slavery

Kittie Productions has zero tolerance for modern slavery and human trafficking. It is the responsibility of all stakeholders, staff, freelancers and contractors to ensure slavery and human trafficking are not taking place within operations or supply chains associated with Kittie Productions.

Due to the diverse organisational make-up of media production, risks may exist within:

  • Production services
  • Materials sourcing
  • External suppliers
  • External contractors

To mitigate risk, reputable suppliers must be sought. Ethical expectations are to be set out in supplier agreements and taken into consideration when selecting preferred bidders.

Kittie Productions and its collaborators must remain vigilant to signs of exploitation and are expected to report any concerns relating to modern slavery. Reports will be taken seriously and handled confidentially.

Anti-Bribery

In compliance with the Bribery Act 2010, Kittie Productions is committed to conducting business ethically. We do not offer, give, request, or accept bribes or improper payments of any kind, and neither should any collaborators, either for their own gain or on behalf of Kittie Productions

Bribes and improper payments can take the form of:

  • Cash
  • Financial incentives
  • Gifts
  • Hospitality
  • Any advantage offered to secure business improperly

Reasonable and proportionate hospitality is permitted where it is transparent and lawful, i.e. when it is not offered to influence decisions.

To avoid conflicts of interest, collaborators must declare any gifts or benefits received in relation to their work with Kittie Productions via the appropriate point of contact, such as a company liaison or line manager.

Any suspected bribery must be reported immediately. Failure to do so may result in disciplinary procedures and, potentially, legal action.

Sustainability

Kittie Productions is committed to reducing the environmental impact of our operations and productions.

Productions will aim to:

  • Minimise waste
  • Reduce unnecessary travel
  • Use digital workflows instead of paper
  • Source materials responsibly
  • Respect external locations

Where possible, we will:

  • Reuse or recycle set materials
  • Work with environmentally responsible suppliers
  • Consider sustainability in production planning

Collaborators should recognise that sustainability is an ongoing process and continually review and improve practices.

Whistleblowing Policy

Kittie Productions encourages a culture of openness and accountability.

Collaborators are encouraged to report concerns about:

  • Illegal activity
  • Unsafe working conditions
  • Harassment or misconduct
  • Ethical breaches

Reports can be made confidentially and without fear of retaliation.

All concerns will be:

  • Taken seriously
  • Investigated appropriately
  • Handled sensitively

No individual will suffer detriment for raising a genuine concern.
